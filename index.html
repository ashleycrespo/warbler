Warbler

History of the font

David Jonathan Ross is a type designer that created Warbler in 2011. Warbler is a unique typeface characterized by its organic, hand-drawn aesthetic and flowing curves. The font's design was inspired by Morris Fuller Benton’s 1928 typeface called Bulmer. David wanted to combined the elegance of calligraphy with a more modern, whimsical twist and create a typeface that exuded both sophistication and playfulness, making it suitable for a variety of design applications, from branding to editorial layouts. The Warbler font family includes multiple weights, allowing for versatility in design while maintaining its distinctive, charming style.
